EVERY PHOTO TELLS A STORY


Storytelling is timeless; an activity that has been taking place for hundreds & thousands of years. Stories are an integral part of human history & culture with written & verbal tales passed down from generation to generation. A photograph will capture & freeze a moment in time whilst conveying an emotion, expressing a mood, sharing an idea & telling a narrative. All key elements of storytelling.


THINGS TO CONSIDER WHEN PLANNING YOUR PHOTO STORY:


  • What is your story about?
  • Who is in your story? Do you need to involve other people?
  • What research is needed? Is your story about a subject you are familiar with?
  • What equipment will you need? With careful planning, almost everything could be shot on a simple phone camera.
  • Where & when are you going to shoot your narrative?

CONSIDER YOUR BEGINNING, MIDDLE & END


  • IDEAS FOR INTRODUCTORY SHOTS

    • A wide landscape shot, setting the scene & showing the location of your story.
    • A suitcase & train ticket illustrating the start of a journey.

  • IDEAS FOR CONCLUSION SHOTS

    1. Sunset at the end of your travels (although this could be considered a cliche shot!)
    2. The blowing out of candles on a birthday cake, the end of a year
      • A happy client


    THEMES


    To keep your story interesting, you should aim for variety in your images. However, you also want visual consistency to create a cohesive story. Consider visual or stylistic themes that you could interweave throughout your story to tie your images together.
